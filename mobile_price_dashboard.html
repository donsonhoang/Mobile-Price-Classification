I used this mobile price classification dataset to investigate which hardware specs actually matter for pricing. After loading it into MySQL, I computed per-tier averages, feature adoption rates, and feature-to-price correlations. The biggest finding: RAM alone explains 91.7% of the price variation — everything else is secondary.

What I Found

RAM is everything
With a 0.917 correlation, RAM alone is nearly a complete predictor of price tier. Budget phones average 785 MB; flagship phones average 3,449 MB — a 4.4× difference.
Battery matters, but less than you'd think
Battery power comes in 2nd with a 0.201 correlation. Flagship phones average 1,380 mAh vs. 1,117 for budget — meaningful, but nothing like the RAM gap.
Screen resolution is 3rd and 4th
Pixel width (0.166) and height (0.149) together form the second-most predictive signal after RAM. Higher price = sharper display, on average.
4G, WiFi, Bluetooth don't predict price
All connectivity features show near-zero correlation with price (0.015–0.021). Budget and flagship phones have virtually the same 4G adoption rate (~52%).
Cameras barely differentiate tiers
Primary camera only goes from 9.6 MP (budget) to 10.2 MP (flagship). Front camera is similarly flat. Camera spec is not how price tiers are set in this dataset.
Clock speed is a noise variable
Clock speed has a -0.007 correlation with price — essentially random. Faster CPUs don't reliably appear in more expensive tiers, which is a surprising data insight.
